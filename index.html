The Battlegrounds

The Courses

Three rounds. Three distinct tests. The Mornington Peninsula at its finest — and most unforgiving.

Round 1
Friday Morning  ·  7:20am First Tee Off

Moonah Links

Legends Course

Designed by Ross Perrett and opened in 2003, the Legends Course consistently ranks as the more popular of the two Moonah Links layouts. Each hole is named after a golfing legend — Australian Open champions alongside international icons like Palmer and Nicklaus. The course opens through valleys and dense stretches of ancient Moonah trees before opening out into wild, rugged bunkering and gently rolling links-style fairways.

Par72
Distance~6,315m
Slope137–141
FormatIndividual Stableford
What to Expect

More forgiving than the Open Course, but still a genuine challenge. Approach shot accuracy is critical — hitting the correct portion of these large, undulating greens is the difference between a makeable putt and a three-putt. The 16th features the largest green in the southern hemisphere, which sounds generous until you're facing a 25-metre lag putt with significant break. The 18th is a 580m par 5 littered with eleven strategically placed bunkers.

Who It Suits

Wide fairways make tee shots forgiving — accuracy off the tee is less critical than approach shot precision and putting on large, undulating greens. Players with a volatile driver but a reliable mid-game will find this layout more forgiving than most. Long hitters with poor short games will struggle to capitalise.

📍 Peter Thomson Dr, Fingal VIC 3939
Round 2
Friday Afternoon  ·  1:20pm First Tee Off

Moonah Links

Open Course

Peter Thomson's magnum opus — the only course in Australia specifically designed and built to host the Australian Open, which it did in 2003 and 2005. Thomson called it "The Leviathan." The layout is routed over rolling sand dunes, with over 80 pot bunkers scattered across the course, many hidden or placed mid-fairway. Blind tee shots feature throughout, and the coastal wind adds another layer of difficulty at every turn.

Par72
Distance~6,783m
Slope143–144
FormatScramble
What to Expect

Championship golf. Five par fours exceed 400m. Three holes stretch beyond 515m. Thomson estimated a club golfer would need an additional eight strokes for net par. The 10th — a 464m par four — plays longer than many par fives, particularly into wind. The scramble format will be essential. One quality shot per position can tame the Leviathan — individual medal play here would be a different story entirely.

Who It Suits

Length matters. The sheer yardage demands players who can move the ball off the tee — teams with at least one long, reliable driver will have a significant advantage. Beyond distance, this course rewards composure. The pot bunkers are penal, the greens multi-tiered, and course management is critical. Local knowledge of where hidden bunkers lie separates good scores from disasters.

📍 Peter Thomson Dr, Fingal VIC 3939
Round 3 — The Decider
Saturday Morning  ·  9:00am Tee Off

St Andrews Beach

Golf Club

Tom Doak's first Australian mainland design, opened in 2004. Doak has stated publicly that if he could have any course in his backyard, this would be it — high praise from an architect whose portfolio includes Pacific Dunes, Ballyneal, and Tara Iti. Set across 175 hectares of former grazing land, routed across sweeping duneland near the thundering surf of Gunnamatta Beach. Doak's minimalist philosophy is on full display — 16 of the 18 fairways were left entirely in their natural state, with virtually no earth moved during construction.

Par70
Distance~6,074m
Slope123–139
FormatIndividual Stableford
What to Expect

A complete contrast to Moonah Links. The layout uses short par 4s and par 3s to navigate the steepest ridges, while longer holes stretch through the valleys. Features Australia's longest par four (13th, 500m) and reportedly its longest par three (16th). The greens are among the smallest and most complex Doak has built. Prevailing winds arrive from the west and southwest off Bass Strait — because the routing forms a loop, the wind changes its relationship to the player on nearly every hole. Expect wallabies on the property, particularly early morning.

Who It Suits

This course rewards the thinker. At just over 6,000m and par 70, raw distance is less important than shot selection and course management. The small, contoured greens demand precision — this is not a course where you miss greens and rely on a straightforward up-and-down. Mentally, this is where the weekend will be decided. Fatigue from Friday's 36 holes will be a factor. The player who can maintain concentration and commit to smart decisions under pressure will be lifting The Vase on Saturday afternoon.

📍 Browns Rd, Fingal VIC 3939

How It Works

Format & Rules

The rules are simple. The handicaps are controversial. The trash talk is mandatory.

Round Venue Format Individual Pts Team Pts Notes
Rd 1 Moonah Links — Legends Stableford 12 – 1 6 – 1 Pairs Aggregate Points awarded to top 12 individually. Pairs combined Stableford score earns team points.
Rd 2 Moonah Links — Open Scramble N/A 6 – 3 – 1 Win / Draw / Loss Team format only. No individual points on offer.

The Snake Draft
The top 4 players from Round 1 become team captains. In rank order, each captain chooses their draft position in the snake — they don't get assigned it.

The snake runs: 1 → 2 → 3 → 4 → 4 → 3 → 2 → 1… and repeats.

Example: The Round 1 leader might elect to draft 4th in the first round — meaning they pick last initially, but first in round 2 of the draft, and last again in the final round. A calculated gamble on who's still available mid-draft.
Rd 3 St Andrews Beach Stableford 16 – 1 8 – 1 Pairs Aggregate ★ Top 8 on the leaderboard randomised with bottom 8 for Saturday pairs. The final shake-up.
🅾

Stableford Scoring

Points awarded based on your score relative to par on each hole — Bogey = 1 pt, Par = 2 pts, Birdie = 3 pts, Eagle = 4 pts. Bad holes cost you nothing; you score zero and move on. Handicap strokes are applied per hole.

↗ Rules of Golf — Stableford (R&A)

🤝

Scramble

All players tee off. The best ball is selected and must remain in place — played as it lies. All other players drop within one club length, no closer to the hole. Repeat until holed.

  • Every player must contribute a minimum of 3 tee shots across the round.
  • A ball in the bunker must be dropped in the bunker, not placed.
  • One club length applies throughout the course — if the drop moves you from rough to fairway, that's your good fortune. The drop may not bring you onto the green.
  • Exception: if the selected ball is in a hazard, all players must play from within that hazard within one club length.
  • Scramble is stroke play. Team handicap calculated via the Scramble Handicap Calculator.
🥇

The Vase

The overall winner is determined by aggregate individual and team points across all three rounds. The player with the most points at the end of Saturday claims The Vase.

🔀

The Saturday Shake-Up

Top 8 randomised with bottom 8 for Round 3 pairs. No hiding. No safe draws. If you're leading, you're getting tested. If you're trailing, this is your lifeline.

🍀

Handicap Integrity

All handicaps are declared prior to the trip and locked in. Current official Golf Australia handicaps apply. Queries go to the committee — decisions are final.

⚽

Nearest the Pin

Each course has 4 par 3s. Open to anyone who wants in.

  • $20 entry per round — $5 per par 3 hole.
  • Closest to the pin on each hole takes that hole's pot.
  • Applies to all three rounds. In the Round 2 scramble, everyone hits their own tee shot on par 3s for nearest the pin purposes.
Jug Rules

Buying Jugs

Certain offences result in the immediate obligation to buy a jug. There is no appeal process.

🍺

Automatic Jug Offences

  • 4-putt on any hole
  • Playing from the ladies tee
  • 4 shots in the sand on a single hole
  • Putting from off the green

* Putts are only counted on the green. Strokes taken from off the green do not count as putts.

🏷

The 3-Putt Token

At the start of Rounds 1 and 3, a token is given to the group. The first player to 3-putt takes possession of the token. The holder of the token at the end of the round buys a jug.

  • To pass the token, the holder must first 2-putt on the same hole as someone else 3-putts.
  • If the holder 2-putts and two or more players 3-putt on the same hole, those players rock paper scissors for the token.
